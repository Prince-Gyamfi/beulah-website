Northern Region Projects

Reaching the Unreached in Northern Region: Compassion in Action

The Northern Region, with its vast rural expanse and scattered villages, has long been underserved despite the unwavering efforts of Kingdom workers. Beulah Foundation has made strategic efforts to extend lasting impact through infrastructure, support, and spiritual outreach in this region, where harsh living conditions often meet great faith and resilience.

Our initiatives here began with the provision of housing and welfare packages for aged pastors in districts such as Tamale, Yendi, and Bunkpurugu. Most of these ministers lived in mud houses with failing roofs and no formal retirement support. The Beulah Housing Project built modest but dignified homes, offering safety, comfort, and ownership to these heroes of faith. In addition, we launched a mobile welfare and evangelism campaign to reach remote communities across the savannah. This included food distribution, basic medical aid, and powerful gospel campaigns in towns like Gushegu and Saboba, where churches were revitalized and souls saved. Children of pastors also benefited from our scholarship support, enabling continued education even in the face of hardship.

Challenge & Solution

The vast distances and difficult terrain of the Northern Region made it challenging to identify, assess, and support target beneficiaries. In response, Beulah Foundation partnered with local pastors and community leaders who served as coordinators, ensuring that help reached those most in need. Our team also utilized motorbikes and vans to access interior settlements that are often unreachable by car.

Final Result

Through compassion-driven projects in the Northern Region, Beulah Foundation has transformed the lives of more than 80 families, established new connections with local churches, and extended the message of Christ into areas previously untouched. Our work continues here with a vision to reach every overlooked village with tangible hope and practical care.
