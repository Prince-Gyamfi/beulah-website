About US

Uplifting Forgotten Kingdom Workers Across Ghana

Beulah Foundation is a nonprofit dedicated to supporting faithful ministers of the Gospel serving in rural Ghana. Through housing, education, monthly support, and long-term pension plans, we aim to restore dignity to those who have selflessly labored in God's vineyard.

Our Mission

To remember and restore the dignity of forgotten Kingdom workers in rural Ghana through practical support, sustainable housing, and education opportunities.

Our Vision

At Beulah Foundation, our vision is to see every faithful Kingdom worker in Ghana’s rural communities honored, supported, and empowered in their latter years. We envision a future where no retired minister faces neglect, but instead lives with dignity, housed in safety, strengthened by monthly support, and surrounded by a community of care and prayer. We aim to extend the legacy of the Gospel by uplifting those who first carried it.

Our History

Beulah Foundation was born out of a heartfelt burden to serve the often-forgotten heroes of the Gospel — retired ministers in Ghana’s rural communities. What began as a small act of compassion in 2021 quickly grew into a nationwide mission. Witnessing the struggles of aging pastors who had sacrificed their lives for the Kingdom, we responded with housing initiatives, monthly financial support, and welfare programs.

Over the years, Beulah has built homes, provided scholarships for ministers' children, supported rural outreaches, and launched sustainable pension funds for Kingdom workers. Each project reflects our deep commitment to honoring those who labored in silence, ensuring their latter days are marked with peace, dignity, and hope.
